Optimal Control of a Differentiable Vocal Tract

Companion page for Grzegorek & Kuczyńska-Grzegorek (submitted to IEEE/ACM TASLP)

This page presents audio samples generated by a differentiable articulatory speech synthesizer. The system models the physics of the human vocal apparatus — glottis, tongue, and throat/lip constrictions — as a differentiable forward pass, and recovers articulatory parameter trajectories from natural speech via gradient-based optimization. Below, each utterance is shown alongside syntheses produced by Adam (2000 iterations) and iLQR (50 iterations).

Model Predictive Control (MPC)

The control formulation enables a receding-horizon MPC mode: at each frame, the system solves a short-horizon iLQR problem and applies only the first control, then re-plans from the new state. This produces causal, streaming synthesis that does not require access to the full target utterance in advance. Online Target Switching

A unique capability of the MPC formulation is mid-utterance target switching: the system begins tracking one acoustic target and seamlessly transitions to a different target partway through the utterance. This is fundamentally impossible with batch optimization (Adam or batch iLQR), which optimizes all frames jointly against a single fixed target. Below, the system starts with valentine and switches to strawberry mid-utterance, using an MPC horizon of 15 frames.
