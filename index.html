Optimal Control of a Differentiable Vocal Tract

Companion page for Grzegorek & Kuczyńska-Grzegorek (submitted to IEEE/ACM TASLP)

This page presents audio samples generated by a differentiable articulatory speech synthesizer. The system models the physics of the human vocal apparatus — glottis, tongue, and throat/lip constrictions — as a differentiable forward pass, and recovers articulatory parameter trajectories from natural speech via gradient-based optimization. Below, each utterance is shown alongside syntheses produced by Adam (2000 iterations) and iLQR (50 iterations).
